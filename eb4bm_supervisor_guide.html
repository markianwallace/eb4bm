Engineering Biology for Biomedicine

A UKRI-BBSRC Centre for Doctoral Training

Supervisor Project Guide

Programme Overview

EB4BM is a 39-student doctoral programme (24 UKRI + 15.5 partner-funded) bringing together King's College London, University of Leeds, and National Physical Laboratory with 18 project partners. The programme trains engineering biologists at the critical interface between fundamental biological design and biomedical implementation.

Core Structure: Linking Domains to Challenges

Every PhD project must connect ONE Engineering Biology Domain with ONE Biomedical Challenge Area through co-supervision. This ensures students develop the interdisciplinary skills that industry partners have identified as critically needed but currently lacking.

Engineering Biology Domains

Choose one domain as the core technical approach:

1. Biomolecular Engineering

Protein engineering, RNA biology, AI-driven design, biomimetic systems for precision therapies.

2. Biomaterials Engineering

Membrane engineering, mechanobiology, nanomaterials for therapeutic delivery and programmable microenvironments.

3. Bioprocessing and Scale-up

Cell culture optimization, nanomaterial manufacturing, scalable purification for engineered biology.

Biomedical Challenge Areas

Choose one challenge area that the project will address:

1. Engineered Cellular Entry

Control of cellular access for engineered biological materials to precise intracellular targets.

2. Programmable Microenvironments

Design of microenvironments using nanomaterials and programmable systems to reprogram cellular niches.

3. Synthetic Cell-to-Cell Communication

Engineer novel cellular signalling pathways for programmable therapeutic outcomes.

Project Requirements

  • Co-supervision: Projects require two supervisors linking an engineering domain with a biomedical challenge
  • Partner involvement: 25% of projects will be CASE partnerships with direct industry co-supervision
  • Translational focus: All projects must address the pathway from design through to implementation, incorporating standards and scale-up considerations
  • NPL integration: Students receive training in metrology and engineering biology standards essential for real-world translation

Example Project Framework

Project: "Nanoneedles for T-cell Manufacturing"

Domain: Biomaterials Engineering (membrane permeability control)

Challenge: Engineered Cellular Entry (efficient transfection)

Application: GMP-compliant gene editing for cell therapies

Supervisors: One biomaterials expert + one cellular therapy specialist

Student Experience

  • Direct-entry to defined projects (83% of students preferred this model)
  • 4-week intensive foundational training
  • Annual summer symposia with all cohorts and partners
  • Structured career development for diverse pathways
  • Industrial placements (all placement costs covered by partners)
  • Regular thesis committee reviews

Timeline

Start: October 2026

Structure: 3 cohorts of ~12 students each

UKRI funding: 8 studentships per year (at least 25% CASE)

Creating Your Project Proposal

When developing projects, consider:

  1. Which engineering domain provides the core technical approach?
  2. Which biomedical challenge will the project address?
  3. How does the project connect fundamental innovation to therapeutic application?
  4. What industry partner expertise could enhance the project?
  5. What pathway exists from lab to implementation?
Project Evaluation Criteria

Project proposals will be evaluated by our Scientific Board for:

  • Alignment with UKRI National Engineering Biology Programme priorities
  • Balance across domains and challenge areas
  • Quality of interdisciplinary supervision plan
  • Partner engagement and translational potential
